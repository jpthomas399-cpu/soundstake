Platform Mechanics

How SoundStake works

Five steps from track upload to payout. Everything that needs to be trusted runs on a smart contract — no one controls the outcome, and every critique is permanent.

01

The problem we're solving

On music upload platforms, artists post their work hoping to get useful feedback. What they usually get is a mix of emojis, one-liners, and "this is fire" — which tells them nothing. The people who actually know what they're talking about have no reason to spend twenty minutes writing a real critique, and the people who don't know anything face zero consequences for wasting an artist's time.

The problem isn't that good listeners don't exist. It's that the platform treats all feedback the same, so there's no point in trying harder.

⚡
The Problem
Feedback quality is low because there's no cost to leaving a bad comment and no reward for leaving a good one. Effort and quality are invisible.
💡
The Idea
What if critics had to put a small amount of money on the line before submitting feedback — and got rewarded when the community agreed it was useful?
In one sentence

SoundStake makes music feedback worth giving — critics have skin in the game, artists get real notes, and the community self-polices.

02

The five-step loop

From track upload to payout, every step is either transparent, on-chain, or both. Here's exactly what happens.

1
Artist uploads a track
Artists post their work and opt it into the staked critique system. The track stays open for feedback for a set period. Opting in is voluntary — artists choose which tracks they want rigorous feedback on.
🎵
2
Critic locks SOUND tokens
To submit feedback, a critic connects their crypto wallet and locks a small amount of SOUND tokens (~$0.50). This stake is held by the smart contract — not us — until the vote closes. No stake, no critique form.
🔐
3
Critic writes real feedback
The critique is submitted and saved to the blockchain via IPFS. It's immutable — no one, including us, can edit or delete it. The critic's wallet address is permanently attached to it.
✍️
4
Community votes for 48 hours
Other users vote on whether the critique was genuinely useful. The vote window is 48 hours. Critics with higher reputation scores carry more voting weight — which means the community polices itself over time.
🗳️
5
Smart contract auto-settles
When voting closes, the smart contract checks the result and settles automatically. 60%+ yes: critic gets tokens back plus a quality bonus. Below 60%: critic loses 20% of their stake. No human step. No appeals. No interference.
⚡
03

Key parameters

Every number in the system was chosen deliberately. Here's what each one is and why it's set where it is.

ParameterValueReason
Minimum stake~$0.50 in SOUND tokensLow enough for students, high enough to make people think twice before submitting a throwaway comment.
Vote window48 hoursEnough time for the community to weigh in across time zones without dragging out resolution.
Approval threshold60% yes-voteSimple majority — clear, transparent, hard to game with sock puppet accounts.
Penalty for bad feedbackLose 20% of stakeMeaningful enough to deter low-effort submissions. Not so high it wipes someone out for one miss.
Reputation badgeNFT stored in walletPermanent, non-sellable, permanently yours. Verifiable by anyone without needing to contact us.
Platform fee5% on settled stakesSplit with the host platform. Passive income from quality engagement — not by charging users more.
BlockchainBase (by Coinbase)Fees at fractions of a cent. A $0.50 stake is only practical because Base keeps costs negligible.
